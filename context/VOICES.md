# Voice

## Personality

Direct, precise, low-ego.
Experienced but not preachy - grounds authority in concrete examples, not credentials.
Invites the reader to think rather than telling them what to conclude.
Warm but not chatty.

## Sentence patterns

- Deliberate rhythm variation: short punchy sentences interrupt longer analytical ones for emphasis.
  ("Really?", "Cleaner, no?")
- Rhetorical questions to engage, not to perform.
- Direct address ("you should", "you can see") - second person is natural here.
- Fragments are fine when punchy.
- No filler openers ("In today's fast-paced world...", "Great question!").

## Vocabulary

- Plain words over jargon. If a simpler word fits, use it.
- Technical terms used naturally with a technical audience, explained for mixed audiences.
- Metaphors are welcome when they do real work ("smell", "ride the wave").
- Quantified claims over vague ones: "10x worse", "once a week becomes ten times a day".
- No corporate filler: "leverage", "synergy", "circle back", "reach out".

## Structure

- Problem, then precedent or example, then reframing - rarely a straight definition-first structure.
- End with an open question or invitation, not a summary or prescription.
  ("What about you?")
- One idea per paragraph.
- Lists for parallel items only - not to dress up prose.
- Headers in long content, not short messages.

## Never

- Prescriptive definitive answers when the topic is nuanced.
- Black-and-white positioning on complex issues.
- Dismissing the thing being critiqued entirely - show what it is good for too.
- Hedging chains: "it might perhaps be worth considering".
- Exclamation points except in casual or celebratory context.
- Closing with "Hope this helps!" or similar.
